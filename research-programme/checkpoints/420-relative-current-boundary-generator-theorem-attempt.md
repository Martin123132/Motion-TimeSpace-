## 9. Decision

Relative-current boundaries can now be stated as a serious candidate-domain generator theorem target: if a parent-owned J_rel=(j_3,b_2) is closed, regular, stress-safe, and equipped with a finite/integrable candidate measure, then candidate domains can be generated before C_exp scoring. This partially repairs the circularity. But the theorem does not yet pass: closure is not physical representative selection, support is not automatically a bound domain, relative topology need not be trivial, and transition exchange remains a retained coefficient source. Therefore this is a candidate-generator contract, not local GR.

Practical read: this partially repairs the domain-candidate problem, but it does not yet select the physical local representative. `J_rel` can draw a theory-native candidate map; it cannot yet tell us which local box is the GR one without the next theorem.
