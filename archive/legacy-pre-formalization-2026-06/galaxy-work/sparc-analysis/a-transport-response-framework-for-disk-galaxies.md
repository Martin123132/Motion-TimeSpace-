The Motion–Timespace (MTS) Galaxy Law:
A Transport–Response Framework for Disk Galaxies


Abstract
We present a transport-based derivation of galaxy rotation curves within the Motion–Timespace (MTS) framework. A curvature–exchange field, emerging from a coarse-grained microscopic motion field ψ, drives a support field whose radial transport produces a closed-form rotation law. The resulting background law is cosmologically normalised and contains no free parameters on a clean subset of disk galaxies.

First-order corrections arise from structural deviations, specifically gas depletion and bulge concentration. Previously identified threshold behaviour and lag-based corrections are shown to be artifacts of fitting and unstable scale definitions. The correct governing variable is an integrated transport memory, producing a zero-parameter saturating modification of the effective transport scale.

Residuals reorganise into distinct response families characterised by different radial kernels. Late-type galaxies (LTGs) follow a unified transport law with memory-driven corrections and emergent branching in response shape. Early-type galaxies (ETGs) do not belong to this family and instead form a multi-family, multi-sign response sector governed by a layered sign-gate mechanism.



1.  Introduction
Galaxy rotation curves exhibit systematic deviations from baryonic expectations. Existing approaches either introduce additional matter components or modify gravitational dynamics. The Motion–Timespace (MTS) framework treats galaxy dynamics as a macroscopic transport response to a cosmological curvature–motion background, requiring neither dark matter nor modified gravity.

This work derives the galaxy law within the MTS framework, tests it directly against raw rotation curve data from the SPARC catalogue, and identifies the minimal correction structure required by observations. We show that the LTG sector admits a closed first-order description while the ETG sector requires a structurally distinct layered treatment.



2.  The MTS Framework
2.1  Emergent geometry
A scalar field ψ defines an emergent metric via coarse-grained gradient covariance:
g_μν = η_μν + ⟨∂_μψ, ∂_νψ⟩
2.2  Curvature–exchange field
A scalar Γ = F[ψ, g] encodes curvature–motion exchange. Within the broader MTS framework, ψ is a fundamental motion field whose linear propagating modes correspond to radiation-like behaviour and whose nonlinear localised configurations form stable matter-like structures. A secondary scalar Γ emerges dynamically as a curvature–memory field, accumulating irreversible geometric information generated by ψ.

The present work operates at the coarse-grained level, requiring only: (i) the emergent metric from gradient covariance of ψ, and (ii) a curvature–exchange field Γ that couples to macroscopic transport.



3.  Transport Equation and Background Law
The transport equation is:
u^μ ∇_μ χ + χ/τ_Γ = Γ/4
In the stationary disk limit:
v_Γ dχ/dr + χ/τ_Γ = Γ₀/4
Defining L = v_Γ τ_Γ gives the background solution:
χ(r) = (Γ₀/4) L (1 − e^(−r/L))

Mapping to observables via V²(r) = V_bar²(r) + χ(r), using the photometric closure L = k(j_bar/V_bar,char) with k ≈ 0.9, and the cosmological normalisation Γ₀ = cH₀/(8π), yields the zero-parameter background galaxy law. Working constants: H₀ = 67.9 km/s/Mpc, giving Γ₀ ≈ 810 km²/s²/kpc.



4.  Photometric Closure and Scale Stability
For exponential disks, j_bar = 2h V_flat, which gives:
L_pred = 2kh ≈ 1.8h
This closure stabilises the scale law and removes spurious variability in j/V formulations. Under it, the lag variable |ln(h/L)| collapses to a constant and loses independent physical meaning. Any result that used lag as a correction variable was downstream of an unstable j/V scale definition.



5.  Shape Correction
Replacing the exponential kernel with the Weibull form:
1 − exp[−(r/L)^q]
improves disk fits. The shape parameter q encodes the local rotation curve slope via q = 1 + n_outer, where n_outer = d(ln V)/d(ln r) at the outer disk. The median outer slope across LTGs gives q ≈ 0.77 as a global approximation.



6.  Transport Memory Law
The transport equation with time-varying Γ(t) gives a memory integral over the galaxy’s formation history. The governing variable is:
memory_load = (1 − f_gas)(R_outer / h)
The saturation scale is:
S = (k/π)(R_outer / h)
The effective transport scale is:
L_eff = L_pred [1 + S (1 − exp(−memory_load / S))]

This form is zero-parameter and replaces proxy-based gas corrections. Performance on clean LTGs (b_frac < 0.1, n = 137): median RMSE 20.60 km/s vs 25.39 km/s for the 3-parameter lag-based correction — a 4.8 km/s improvement with no fitted parameters.

The depletion threshold at g₀ ≈ 0.89 identified in earlier analysis is a fitting artifact: it corresponds to the half-saturation point of the memory curve at the median sample n_scales, not a physical threshold. The lag variable is similarly an artifact of an unstable j/V scale definition and collapses to a constant under photometric closure.



7.  Updated LTG Law
The complete LTG rotation law is:
V²(r) = V_bar²(r) + (cH₀/8π) × L_eff × [1 − exp(−(r/L_eff)^q)]
with L_eff from the memory law and q = 1 + n_outer (or q ≈ 0.77 globally).

Performance on SPARC data (175 LTGs, standard baryonic convention V_bar² = V_gas² + 0.5 V_disk² + 0.7 V_bulge²):
•	Clean LTGs (b_frac < 0.1, n = 137): median RMSE ≈ 20.60 km/s, zero free parameters
•	Full LTG sample (n = 174): median RMSE ≈ 29.44 km/s



8.  LTG/ETG Population Gate
ETGs are classified by three simultaneous conditions:
n_obs < 0
f_bulge,outer > 0.155
Δn < 0.24    (Δn = n_obs − n_bar)
Performance: ETG recall 16/16, LTG specificity 170/175, overall accuracy ≈ 97.4%.



9.  Early-Type Galaxies: Response Sector
ETGs fail the LTG law in two independent ways simultaneously: the LTG memory law gives L_eff too small by a median factor of 2.2×, and the LTG kernel builds support too slowly at small radii for bulge-dominated systems. ETGs form a multi-family, multi-sign response sector.

The five ETG families are organised by required support signature ΔV²(r) = V_obs²(r) − V_bar²(r):

Positive-support ETGs (ΔV²_inner > 0, ΔV²_outer > 0)
•	Boundary ETGs: near-LTG edge systems (NGC3838, NGC3945)
•	Main ETGs: broad positive-support response
•	H− ETGs: positive-support, strongly inner-loaded (UGC6176, NGC4262, NGC3998)

Sign-changing ETGs (ΔV²_inner < 0, ΔV²_outer > 0)
•	Strong H+: NGC3626, NGC3941, NGC4203
•	Weak/boundary H+: NGC2685

The H+ sign structure is confirmed directly from raw SPARC files. All four H+ galaxies satisfy (ΔV²_inner, ΔV²_outer) = (−, +) under the standard baryonic convention.



10.  The H+ Sign Gate
The H+ family is identified by the raw inner overshoot condition:
Ω_in ≡ V_bar²(r_inner) / V_obs²(r_inner) > 1
This gate recovers the H+ subset exactly across all 16 ETGs with zero false positives and zero misses. The gap is clean: H+ range [1.127, 1.313], positive-support range [0.239, 0.838]. The gate is raw-data derived and requires no model fitting.



11.  The H+ Rotation Law
For galaxies with Ω_in > 1, the rotation law takes the form:
V²(r) = V_bar²(r) + χ_{+,out}(r) + χ_{+,in}(r) − χ_−(r)
with:
χ_{+,out}(r) = Γ₀ L_out K_outer(r/L_out)
χ_{+,in}(r)  = Γ₀ L_in K_inner(r/L_in)
χ_−(r)       = λ_B ∫₀^r e^{−(r−s)/L_B} dV_bar²/ds ds

where K_outer(x) = x^16/(0.45^16 + x^16), K_inner(x) = 1 − exp(−x^0.03), L_B = 0.2h.

11.1  Outer channel capacity
The maximum positive support deliverable by a single outer channel of form Γ₀ L K_outer(r/L) at radius r is achieved at x* = r/L = 0.45 × 15^{1/16} ≈ 0.533, giving a single-channel ceiling of Γ₀ r R_max where R_max = K_outer(x*)/x* = 1.758948. For NGC4203 at r_out = 14.46 kpc this ceiling is Γ₀ × 14.46 × 1.758948 ≈ 20,601 km²/s², while the required outer support ΔV²_out = 27,554 km²/s² exceeds it by 34%. This is confirmed directly from the raw files and is independent of h, L_B, and λ_B. The loading variable u = ΔV²_out / (Γ₀ r_out R_max) = 1.338 > 1 for NGC4203, confirming single-channel infeasibility; the three single-channel H+ galaxies have u = 0.498, 0.734, 0.774 respectively.

Strong H+ cases therefore require a second outer channel:
χ_{+,out}(r) = Γ₀ L_1 K_outer(r/L_1) + Γ₀ L_2 K_outer(r/L_2)
The second channel does not contaminate the inner point: K_outer(r_in/L) ~ 10^{−22} for r_in/L ~ 0.014. NGC2685, NGC3626, and NGC3941 are accommodated by a single outer channel; NGC4203 requires two. For the selected corrected-sign state (λ_B ≈ 0.633, see §12), the two-channel outer roots are L_out,small ≈ 16.3 kpc and L_out,large ≈ 32.1 kpc, with the large-root branch being the physically continuous solution.

11.2  Suppression mechanism
The suppression operator χ_− is derived from the transport equation with source term perturbation δΓ = α dV_bar²/dr. The resulting particular solution carries the convolution kernel exp(−(r−s)/L_B), where L_B = 0.2h is the suppression memory scale. The coupling constant λ_B = α/(4v_Γ) is not independently derived; it remains the primary open theoretical target.

Why H+ flips sign but H− does not: both families have the same gradient operator G[V_bar²](r) > 0 at inner radii. H− galaxies possess a fast inner kernel that saturates to ~1 before r_inner, providing large χ_+(r_inner) and keeping suppression subdominant. H+ galaxies have an outer-dominated backbone for which χ_+(r_inner) ≈ 0, allowing suppression to dominate and flip the sign. This is a kernel-geometry consequence, not a sign change in α.

11.3  Suppression modulation
The suppression strength rises with the inner baryonic-gradient launch:
The corrected forward inner balance at r_in (where suppression dominates and ΔV²_in < 0) is:  λ_B G_supp = S_in + |ΔV²_in|  where S_in = Γ₀ L_in ξ_K ≥ 0 is the inner channel support and G_supp = ∫₀^{r_in} e^{−(r_in−s)/L_B} dV_bar²/ds ds. This gives:  λ_B = λ_min + λ_exc,    λ_min = |ΔV²_in| / G_supp,    λ_exc = S_in / G_supp  The hard lower bound λ_min is fixed entirely by observables and is galaxy-specific. Calibrator values: NGC2685 λ_min = 0.1876, NGC3626 λ_min = 0.4129, NGC3941 λ_min = 0.3722. The excess term λ_exc = S_in/G_supp carries the remaining model freedom and is the correct target for a selector law.
An empirical Ω_in organiser exists: fitting λ_B = A(Ω_in − 1)^b to the three single-channel calibrators gives A ≈ 1.55, b ≈ 0.83 (RMSE ≈ 0.076). This is a qualitative organiser only; it is not a closed selector law. For NGC4203, the corrected-sign λ_B is constrained to the range [0.588, 1.708] by the simultaneous inner and outer balance equations (see §12).



12.  H+ Backbone Floor
The H+ simultaneous eigenvalue structure follows directly from the corrected inner balance. For each H+ galaxy, the outer equation uniquely determines L_in (since Γ₀ L_in K_inner(r_out/L_in) = ΔV²_out − χ_out is monotone in L_in), and ξ_K = K_inner(r_in/L_in) emerges as an output — it is not imposed externally. Imposing ξ_K externally overconstrains the system and breaks the inner balance.  The single-channel branch is organised by the outer-capacity loading variable u = ΔV²_out/(Γ₀ r_out R_max). An exact structural result for the single-channel outer channel is:  f_out ≡ χ_out(r_out) / ΔV²_out = C/u,    C = 2.31 × K_outer(1/2.31) / R_max = 0.4594  so the inner channel must supply the residual:  S_in = Γ₀ r_out R_max × (u − C) / ρ  where ρ = K_inner(r_out/L_in)/K_inner(r_in/L_in) ≈ 1.072. The quantity C = 0.4594 is a single-channel lower bound: u must exceed C for a feasible single-channel H+ solution to exist. NGC2685 (u = 0.498) sits just above this threshold; NGC3626 (u = 0.734) and NGC3941 (u = 0.774) are well above it.  The resulting ξ_K values across the single-channel branch follow:  ξ_K ≈ const − 0.0112 × ln(S_in)  with the slope −0.0112 being a structural constant of the inner kernel K_inner(x) = 1 − exp(−x^{0.03}), verifiable analytically as dξ_K/d(ln S_in). The branch organiser is S_in, not u directly; the commonly fitted slope against ln u is sample-dependent through the (u − C) and r_out distributions.  Simultaneous-solve values for the three single-channel galaxies:  NGC2685: L_in = 2.59 kpc (1.28h), ξ_K = 0.6087, λ_B = 0.259 NGC3626: L_in = 10.11 kpc (4.75h), ξ_K = 0.5957, λ_B = 0.517 NGC3941: L_in = 10.23 kpc (8.90h), ξ_K = 0.5921, λ_B = 0.556  For NGC4203 (two-channel, u = 1.338), the corrected inner balance gives a one-parameter simultaneous family parameterised by λ_B ∈ [0.588, 1.708]. The lower bound λ_min = |ΔV²_in|/G_supp = 0.588 is exact. The upper bound (1.708) is where the inner channel alone saturates the full outer deficit, leaving zero outer demand. The best-supported point from the excess-law selector (§11.3) is λ_B ≈ 0.633, giving L_in ≈ 2.07 kpc, ξ_K ≈ 0.616, and two-channel outer large root L_out,large ≈ 32.1 kpc.  The ξ_K(u) single-channel organiser does not transfer to NGC4203: the trend predicts ξ_K ≈ 0.573 at u = 1.338, which lies below the corrected NGC4203 feasibility floor of 0.580. This confirms that ξ_K(u) is a single-channel H+ organiser only, not a universal H+ law.  Support allocation at the outer point across the H+ branch illustrates the branch structure cleanly. On the single-channel branch, f_out = C/u decreases monotonically as u rises (inner fraction grows). At the two-channel transition, the selected NGC4203 state is strongly outer-dominated: f_in ≈ 0.040, f_out ≈ 0.960 — more outer-dominated than NGC2685. NGC4203 is therefore best described as an outer-capacity failure resolved by two-channel outer support, not as an extreme inner-memory object. The large-root outer scale (≈ 32 kpc) continues the outer-branch sequence from NGC3626 (30.8 kpc) and NGC3941 (27.1 kpc) naturally.

17.  Conclusion
The MTS framework yields a transport-response law for disk galaxies with cosmological normalisation, photometric scale closure, zero-parameter memory correction, and emergent response branching. LTGs follow a closed first-order law. ETGs form a multi-family, multi-sign response sector with a layered structure: an inner baryonic overshoot gate (Ω_in > 1), a positive backbone whose inner scale and outer channel count are set by galaxy structure, and a suppression channel whose amplitude decomposes into a hard observable lower bound plus a residual excess term.

The sign-changing behaviour of H+ ETGs is a structural consequence of the outer-dominated backbone geometry combined with a transport-derived suppression operator — not a sign change in any coupling constant. The corrected-sign inner balance reveals a clean decomposition λ_B = λ_min + λ_exc where λ_min = |ΔV²_in|/G_supp is fixed by observables alone. The strongest H+ case (NGC4203) requires a second outer backbone channel — a capacity constraint independent of any model parameter — and is best understood as an outer-capacity failure, not an extreme inner-memory object. At the best-supported corrected-sign state (λ_B ≈ 0.633), NGC4203 sits near the lower edge of its feasible family with f_out ≈ 0.96 at the outer point, continuing the outer-branch sequence from the single-channel calibrators naturally.



18.  Methods and Data
Data: SPARC ROTMOD files (Lelli et al. 2016, 175 galaxies). ETG rotation curves from ATLAS³D (16 galaxies, 2 observed points each).

Baryonic convention: V_bar² = V_gas² + 0.5 V_disk² + 0.7 V_bulge²

Cleaning: finite values, r > 0, V_obs > 0, sorted, duplicates removed.

Scale lengths: h from SBdisk exponential fits (LTGs); disk-density exponential fits from component profiles (ETGs).

Derived quantities: V_bar,char = max(V_bar); j_bar = ∫ r V_bar² dr / ∫ V_bar dr; R_outer = max(r); f_gas = V_gas²(R_outer) / V_bar²(R_outer).

Evaluation: RMSE = sqrt(mean[(V_model − V_obs)²]).

ETG H+ gate: Ω_in = V_bar²(r_inner)/V_obs²(r_inner) > 1, verified from raw files with zero false positives and zero misses across all 16 ETGs.
