---------------------------------------------------------
ABSTRACT
---------------------------------------------------------

We present a complete, parameter-minimal quantitative analysis of galaxy
rotation curves using the Motion–TimeSpace (MTS) geometric framework.
Across a 20-galaxy SPARC subset spanning three decades in baryonic mass,
we test the hypothesis that galactic dynamics derive from a geometry
in which stiffness m(r) controls curvature-memory storage in space.

This framework produces three central predictions:

1. A universal core-radius scaling:
       R_s(M) = A_R * M^β_R     with β_R ≈ 0.28.

2. A universal mastercurve for all galaxies:
       V/V_inf = 1 − exp[ − ( r / (x0 R_s) )^ν ]
   with ν ≈ 0.94 and x0 ≈ 0.97.

3. A mass-dependent “effective MOND acceleration scale”:
       a0_eff = V_inf^4 / (G M)

We perform 22 tests (forward predictions, BTFR fits, residual structure,
stiffness–shape correlations, and consistency checks). Results:

• The observed BTFR slope (0.31) emerges naturally.
• Pure geometry predicts V_inf with ~9% error.
• Normalised velocity curves collapse to a single mastercurve with RMS 0.060.
• Residuals show no dependence on mass, gas fraction, surface density or size.
• A stiffness contrast Δm controls higher-order shape structure.
• An effective MOND a0 naturally increases with baryonic mass.

We conclude that MTS geometry reproduces the large-scale phenomenology of
galaxy rotation without dark matter halos or modified gravity, and with
predictive structure beyond MOND.

---------------------------------------------------------

Rotation curves remain a major problem in astrophysics. Dark-matter halos
provide fits but lack predictive geometry. MOND fits curves but requires a

MTS approaches the problem differently: geometry has a stiffness field m(r)
that controls how curvature and energy propagate. This produces:

• a cumulative stiffness integral that defines a core radius,

This paper rigorously tests these predictions.

2. METHODS
---------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Exponential Core Fits  
For each galaxy we use:
   V(r) = V_inf * (1 − exp(−b r))
   R_s = 1/b

This gives:
   V_inf  = asymptotic velocity
   R_s    = core scale
   ν_i    = per-galaxy shape exponent (Test 17)

2.2 MTS Forward Model Components:
1. Amplitude law (BTFR):
      V_inf = k * M^0.311

2. Core-radius law (Test 12):
      R_s(M) = 1.402 * M^0.279

3. Universal shape:
      V/V_inf = 1 − exp(−(r/(0.966 R_s))^0.942)

Combining these yields the complete rotation curve prediction
with no per-galaxy parameters.

   (Δm / Δm_ref)^γ    with γ ≈ −0.29

Scatter:

3.4 Test 16 — Residual Structure

Residual RMS shows:
• No correlation with mass
• No correlation with gas fraction
• No correlation with effective surface density
• No correlation with R_s

Only significant correlation:

Close to BTFR-only, but physically structured.

3.7 Test 20 — Identity Check

Compute:

a0_eff increases with mass → explains why BTFR slope = 0.31, not 0.25.
